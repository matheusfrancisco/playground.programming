1. Solve in clusters, not randomly. Pick one topic say, binary search on the answer  and grind 15-20 problems on it back to back. Don't move on until the pattern feels obvious. Random practice builds shallow recognition; clustered practice burns the pattern in.
2. The 20-minute rule. Think for 20 minutes. If stuck, read the editorial. Don't grind for 3 hours on a problem above your level you're just reinforcing confusion. Read the solution, understand why that approach was the right call, then re-implement from scratch without looking.
3. Reverse-engineer the signal. After solving (or reading the solution), ask: "What in the problem statement should have told me to use this?" Write it down. This is the single highest-leverage habit. You're building your own signal → pattern dictionary.
4. Solve slightly above your level. If you're 1200-rated, do 1300-1400 problems. Comfort zone = no growth. Way above zone = no learning, just frustration.
5. Re-solve old problems. A week later, redo problems you struggled with. If the approach doesn't come within 2 minutes, the pattern isn't internalized yet.
